This website is a\u00a0forum for research and scholarship\u00a0on\u00a0early\u00a0German film culture and theory. Building on the\u00a0Promise of Cinema<\/em>\u00a0sourcebook<\/a>, the site offers an archive of digitized film-theoretical\u00a0books<\/a>\u00a0and\u00a0film\u00a0journals<\/a>\u00a0before 1933, links to\u00a0films<\/a>\u00a0from the period, new\u00a0translations<\/a>\u00a0of documents, as well as\u00a0suggestions<\/a>\u00a0for teaching Weimar cinema\u00a0through the lens of theory. There are also\u00a0illustrations of film-technological\u00a0devices<\/a>\u00a0and links to extensive online\u00a0resources<\/a>.\u00a0In addition, the site features original\u00a0essays<\/a>\u00a0by scholars that engage with early German film theory, and a lexicon of media-historical\u00a0concepts\u00a0and constellations<\/a>. Materials relating to the sourcebook itself are found\u00a0here<\/a>. We are keen to bring together in one place all relevant materials (books, journals, illustrations) that deal with German film theory in the broadest sense. If you know of online sources and resources that are missing on our site, please contact<\/a>\u00a0the editors. Any and all contributions to our online research community are welcome.<\/p>\r\n
